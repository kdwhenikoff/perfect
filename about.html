Perfect showcases the less than perfect or "human" results often omitted from the glossy, orderly, nailed-it, socially-networked world of the web. We don't see the dumpster of mistakes before the final, perfect photo or polished write up. But, there is beauty -- and certainly humor -- in bad results.

The goal is doing, not being paralyzed by the fear of a bad result. So, we celebrate bad doing. Besides, a bad result is often a more interesting story than perfection. The ability to laugh at oneself is an important life skill; and sharing these moments increases the fun (and the lesson).
